# Facilitator Guide

You don't need to be an AI expert to run this. You need this guide, the deck, and a
willingness to say "great question ‚Äî let's ask Claude and find out" (which is itself
the lesson).

## Before the session (checklist)

- [ ] Send the invite email (template below) 3+ days ahead.
- [ ] Test the room: projector, wifi, your own Claude account logged in.
- [ ] Skim the [troubleshooting FAQs](../troubleshooting/) ‚Äî they answer 80% of
      hands-up moments.
- [ ] Open the [guide](../guide/index.html) in a tab ‚Äî its quizzes make good
      audience-participation moments.
- [ ] Deep dive only: have participants clone/download this repo in advance.

**Invite email template:**

```text
Subject: [Workshop] Getting real work done with Claude ‚Äî bring a laptop

Before the session, please: (1) create a free account at claude.ai,
(2) [deep dive only] install Node.js from nodejs.org (the LTS version), and
(3) bring one real task from your life ‚Äî an email you're avoiding, a document
to improve, something to study. We'll work on real things, not toy examples.
```

## What trips people up (be ready)

1. **"It gave me a wrong answer, so it's useless."** Reframe: it's a fast junior
   colleague, not an oracle. Verification is part of the workflow, not a failure of it.
2. **Account/login friction eats time.** That's why it's in the invite email; have a
   buddy-up rule (share a screen) for anyone stuck.
3. **The over-trusters worry me more than the skeptics.** Plant the citation-
   verification habit hard (Module 10).
4. **"Is this cheating?"** Don't dodge: policies differ by school/employer; the
   skill is knowing yours and using AI to learn, not to bypass learning. Exercise 4
   exists for this conversation.
5. **Someone asks something you can't answer.** Model the behavior: ask Claude, live,
   on the projector, and evaluate its answer together. This is often the session's
   most memorable moment.

## Accessibility & inclusion notes

- The pairs format in Exercise 1 protects people who'd never type into an AI alone
  in public.
- Never require anyone to share personal task content in share-outs; "describe what
  changed, not what it was about" is the rule.
