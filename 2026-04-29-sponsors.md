# Campus Bike-Share at Four Midwestern Universities: A Survey Study
*(Fictional sample article for practice purposes — Journal of Campus Mobility, 2025)*

**Method.** We surveyed 1,840 students across four midwestern universities with
bike-share programs, asking about usage frequency, trip purpose, and satisfaction.
**Definition note:** we define "usage" as having taken at least one bike-share trip
in the past semester.

**Findings.** By our definition, 61% of respondents are bike-share users. Usage is
strongly associated with living more than a 15-minute walk from central campus.
Satisfaction is high (4.1/5), with complaints concentrated on dock availability at
peak class-change times.

**Limitations.** Self-reported survey data; the four campuses are flat and
bike-friendly, which may not generalize.

**Conclusion.** Bike-share reaches a majority of students and primarily substitutes
for walking, not driving.
