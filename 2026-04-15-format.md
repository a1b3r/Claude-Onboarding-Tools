# Dock Telemetry Evidence on Student Bike-Share Adoption
*(Fictional sample article for practice purposes — Transportation Data Letters, 2026)*

**Method.** We analyzed 14 months of trip-level dock telemetry from one large public
university's bike-share system, matched to anonymized rider accounts.
**Definition note:** we define "usage" as averaging at least one trip per week over
the semester — regular use, not trial use.

**Findings.** Only 18% of student account holders meet our usage bar; the median
account takes 3 trips and goes dormant. A small core (7% of accounts) generates 71%
of all trips. Trips cluster on the class-change peaks.

**Limitations.** One campus; telemetry can't capture why riders lapse.

**Conclusion.** Bike-share is a niche habit sustained by a small core of regulars,
not a mass-adoption service.
