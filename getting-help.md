# Prompts: Career & Job Search

> 📖 Pairs with [Module 08](../guide/index.html#m8). Tip: build a **job-search Project**
> (resume + voice instructions uploaded once) and run these inside it.

## The honest resume tailor
*Attach your resume and paste the posting. Note the guardrail.*

```text
Here's my resume and a job posting. List the top 5 requirements of this role,
map my actual experience to each one, and rewrite my bullet points to speak
to them directly — without inventing or exaggerating anything I didn't do.
```

## The mock interviewer

```text
You're the hiring manager for this role [paste posting]. Ask me six likely
interview questions, one at a time. After each of my answers, give honest
feedback using the STAR framework (Situation, Task, Action, Result) and
suggest one concrete improvement.
```

## The cover letter that doesn't sound like AI

```text
Write a cover letter for [role] at [company]. Use my resume (attached) and
these 2–3 specific things that genuinely interest me about this company:
[your reasons]. Under 300 words, confident but not arrogant, no clichés like
"I'm passionate about" or "fast-paced environment." It should sound like a
sharp person wrote it quickly, not like a template.
```

## The "tell me about yourself" builder

```text
Help me build a 60-second "tell me about yourself" answer for [type of role].
My background: [2–3 sentences]. Structure it as present → past → future.
Then ask me to say it back in my own words and critique my version.
```

## The cold outreach drafter

```text
Draft a short LinkedIn message to [role, e.g., "an alum who works in data
analytics at a company I admire"]. Goal: a 15-minute chat, not a job ask.
Three sentences max, one specific reason I'm reaching out to them
in particular: [reason]. No flattery, no "I hope this finds you well."
```

## The offer decoder
*For when you actually get one.*

```text
Here are the details of a job offer I received: [salary, location, benefits,
role]. Help me build a list of clarifying questions to ask before accepting,
and explain in plain English what each benefit term means. Don't tell me
whether to accept — give me the information to decide.
```
