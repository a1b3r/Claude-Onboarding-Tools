# Do Campus Bike-Shares Pay Off? A Cost-Benefit Analysis
*(Fictional sample article for practice purposes — Higher Ed Operations Review, 2025)*

**Method.** Budget and operations data from nine campus programs, comparing total
cost per trip against a parking-demand counterfactual.

**Findings.** Median cost per trip is $2.10. Programs near break-even share three
traits: dense docks near dorms, fall-semester onboarding pushes, and integration with
student ID cards. Three of nine programs cost over $5 per trip and were judged poor
value.

**Limitations.** Parking counterfactual relies on planning-office estimates;
campuses self-selected into sharing data.

**Conclusion.** Viability is an operations problem: program design choices, not
student demand, separate the successes from the failures.
