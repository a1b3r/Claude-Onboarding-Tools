# Prompts: Writing & Editing

> 📖 Pairs with [Module 04](../guide/index.html#m4). Golden rule: Claude is a better
> *editor of your draft* than ghostwriter of your ideas.

## The tough editor

```text
Critique this draft like a tough but fair editor. Identify the three weakest
points and explain exactly how to fix each one. Do not compliment me — I want
the problems.
```

## The keep-my-voice reviser

```text
Here's my 1,200-word draft. Keep my voice and my argument, but tighten wordy
sentences and flag any paragraph where the logic is weak. List every change
you made at the end so I can review them.
```

## The outline-first drafter
*Best for: starting from a blank page without surrendering the thinking.*

```text
I need to write [type of document] about [topic] for [audience]. Don't write
it yet. First, ask me the 3–5 questions you need answered to outline it well.
After I answer, propose an outline and wait for my approval before drafting
anything.
```

## The tone shifter

```text
Rewrite this message to be [more formal / more direct / warmer / shorter]
while keeping every factual point intact: [paste message]. Show the result,
then list what you changed in one line.
```

## The clarity test

```text
Read this paragraph and tell me: (1) what you think I'm trying to say, in one
sentence, and (2) where a reader could misunderstand me. If your one-sentence
version doesn't match my intent, the paragraph has failed — help me fix it.
```

## The anti-padding pass
*For hard word/page limits.*

```text
This draft is [X] words and the limit is [Y]. Cut it down by removing
redundancy, filler, and throat-clearing — not by removing substance. Mark
anything you cut that you think I might actually want to keep.
```
