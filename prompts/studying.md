# Prompts: Studying & Learning

> 📖 Pairs with [Module 09](../guide/index.html#m9). Pattern: role · process · standard · guardrail.

## The Socratic study partner
*Best for: exam prep. Active recall beats re-reading.*

```text
I'm studying [topic] for [exam/certification]. Quiz me one question at a time
and wait for my answer. After each answer, tell me what I got right or wrong
and why, and make the questions harder as I improve. Don't accept vague
answers — push me to be precise.
```

## The explainer ladder
*Best for: a concept that won't click.*

```text
Explain [concept] three times: first to a smart high schooler, then to a
college senior in [your major], then at expert level. End with one exam-style
question to test whether I really got it.
```

## The misconception hunter
*Best for: before an exam, after you think you know the material.*

```text
I think I understand [topic]. Here's my explanation of it in my own words:
[your explanation]. Identify every error, gap, or misconception in my
explanation — be specific about what's wrong and what the correct version is.
Don't soften it.
```

## The worked-example coach
*Best for: problem-based subjects (math, stats, econ, chemistry).*

```text
Here's a problem I got wrong: [problem + your attempt]. Don't just give me
the answer. First identify where my attempt went off track, then walk me
through the correct approach step by step, then give me one similar problem
to try on my own.
```

## The lecture distiller
*Best for: turning raw notes into something usable. Attach your notes.*

```text
Here are my lecture notes. Turn them into: (1) a one-paragraph summary,
(2) the 5 most likely exam concepts with one-line definitions, and (3) three
practice questions with answers hidden at the bottom. Keep my professor's
terminology.
```

> **Integrity note:** these prompts are built for *learning* the material, not bypassing
> it. Check your institution's AI policy — guide, [Module 10](../guide/index.html#m10).
