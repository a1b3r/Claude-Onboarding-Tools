# Workflow 04 — Research Synthesis

**Situation:** several articles/papers on one topic need to become a structured
briefing that compares them honestly.
**Practice data:** `sample-files/04-research-articles/` — four short (fictional)
articles about campus bike-share programs. Copy the folder out of the repo first.

## The brief

```text
Read the four articles in this folder and write briefing.md:

1. The question they all address, in one sentence
2. A comparison table: source, method, main finding, stated limitation
3. Where the sources agree
4. Where they disagree, and your best explanation of why (different methods?
   different populations? different definitions?)
5. What's still unknown

Rules:
- Attribute every claim to its source by filename.
- Don't smooth over disagreements to make a cleaner story — disagreement is
  a finding.
- Where you make an inference of your own, label it "Inference:" explicitly.
```

## Expected outcome

A briefing that makes the disagreement between sources visible and explains it,
rather than averaging everything into mush.

## Review checklist

- [ ] The planted trap: two articles reach opposite conclusions partly because they
      define "usage" differently. The briefing should catch the definition mismatch.
- [ ] Every claim has a source attribution; "Inference:" labels appear where Claude
      went beyond the sources.
- [ ] Read section 4 — would you trust this person in a seminar discussion?

**The transferable rule:** synthesis ≠ averaging. Asking explicitly for disagreements
and their causes is what produces analysis instead of summary soup.
