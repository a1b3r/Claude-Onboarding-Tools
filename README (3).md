# Prompts: Everyday Life

> 📖 Pairs with [Module 02](../guide/index.html#m2). Post-grad life has a lot of
> first-times. These help.

## The email un-staller
*For the reply you've been avoiding for three days.*

```text
I need to reply to this email: [paste]. What I actually want to say is:
[blunt version]. Write a version that's professional and warm but still says
it clearly. Keep it under 120 words.
```

## The decision clarifier

```text
I'm deciding between [option A] and [option B]. Here's my situation:
[context]. Don't tell me what to choose. Instead: (1) list what each option
optimizes for, (2) identify the assumption my hesitation depends on, and
(3) tell me what information would make this decision easy.
```

## The jargon translator
*Leases, benefits enrollment, insurance, taxes.*

```text
Explain this document to me like I'm signing it tomorrow and you're a friend
who happens to be an expert: [paste/attach]. What does each section actually
mean for me, what's standard, and what should I ask questions about before
signing? (I know you're not my lawyer — I'll verify anything important.)
```

## The meal-plan realist

```text
Plan 5 weeknight dinners for someone with [skill level], [equipment], a
budget of [X]/week, and about [Y] minutes to cook. Reuse ingredients across
meals so nothing gets wasted. Give me one consolidated grocery list.
```

## The trip skeleton

```text
I have [N days] in [place] with a budget of [X], and I care most about
[interests]. Build a day-by-day skeleton with one anchor activity per day
and free space around it. Flag anything that needs booking ahead.
```

## The week un-chaoser

```text
Here's everything on my plate this week: [brain dump]. Sort it into:
must-do-with-deadline, should-do, and can-wait. Then propose a realistic
schedule that assumes things take 1.5x longer than I think.
```
